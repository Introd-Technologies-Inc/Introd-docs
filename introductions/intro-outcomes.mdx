Outcomes are one of the strongest truth sources in Introd. They convert theory into operating data.

![Opportunity flow in Introd](/images/product/opportunity-flow.png)

## Where outcomes flow

- intro request status changes
- intro request event records
- graph sync for outcome ingestion
- connector execution insight invalidation
- relationship intelligence recalculation
- intro outcome and marketplace dashboards

## What outcomes should influence

- connector ranking
- introducer likelihood
- target responsiveness
- company memory and route health
- recommendations for similar future targets
- follow-up sequencing

## Outcome quality bar

The system should distinguish between:

- sent
- accepted
- declined
- replied
- meeting booked
- converted
- ghosted or ignored

Those are not equivalent signals.

## Why this matters to ranking

Connector score, target score, and introducer likelihood already use weighted outcome history in code. That means a connector with fast replies and completed intros should outrank a connector with only generic adjacency.

The product should surface outcome learning back into:
