---
title: "Connector Ranking"
description: "How Introd should rank connectors for warm introductions."
---

Connector ranking should optimize for introduction quality, not just graph distance.

## Ranking inputs

The most useful signals are:

- response rate
- intro success rate
- meeting conversion rate
- reliability score
- freshness and decay
- relationship memory and evidence signals
- context fit for the requested goal

## Practical interpretation

| Signal | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Reliability | A connector who often follows through is more valuable than one with broad but weak reach |
| Freshness | Old relationships should rank lower unless there is new evidence |
| Outcome history | Prior intro outcomes are stronger than inferred similarity |
| Context fit | The best connector can vary by investor ask, customer intro, hiring, or partnership goal |

## What users should see

- who ranked first
- why they ranked first
- what would make another connector stronger
- whether the recommended connector is overused or cooling down
