---
title: "Company Intelligence"
description: "Understand why a company matters and what network evidence makes it reachable."
---

Company intelligence turns a target account into a reachable opportunity map. A good company page should help a user decide whether the account is worth pursuing now and how to enter it credibly.

![Company search and intelligence in Introd](/images/product/company-search.png)

## What this view should answer

- why this company matters right now
- which roles are worth targeting first
- whether the network has real access or only thin coverage
- what evidence makes the account reachable
- which filters produced the current result set
- which saved search or collection should hold the account

## Search and filters

Use company search filters to narrow the result set:

| Filter | Use it for |
| --- | --- |
| Industry | Focus a market such as technology, fintech, healthcare, or real estate. |
| Company size | Separate startup, mid-market, and enterprise workflows. |
| Headquarters | Prioritize local or regional access. |
| Growth stage | Distinguish emerging companies from mature accounts. |
| Technologies | Find accounts using tools relevant to your offer or ecosystem. |

## Results table

The results table should show company identity, industry, headquarters, your connections, confidence, and actions. The most important field is not company name. It is whether Introd can show credible access.

## Side-panel signals

The right-side panels should explain search confidence, top industries, smart filters, and useful help. Use them to understand why a result set looks the way it does before adding accounts to a collection.

## Output quality bar

The company view should read like account strategy, not a scraped directory dump. If the view cannot explain reachability, it is not ready for an intro workflow.
