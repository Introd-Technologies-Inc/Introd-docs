Connector analysis at the company level should still resolve down to a person with a real chance of replying. Company coverage without a connector decision is just research.

![Company search in Introd](/images/product/company-search.png)

## Useful signals

- reachable people count at the account
- strength of the best connector for each target
- evidence density across the account
- department fit for the intro goal
- outcome history into the account

## Relevant response fields

The current company search and graph layers already surface the right primitives:

- `strongestPath`
- `bestConnectorInsight`
- `companyMemory`
- `topRoles`
- `introHistoryCount`
- `relationshipDensity`

## What to rank highest

Choose the connector who can move the right conversation, not the connector with the most generic adjacency.

In practice that means favoring:

- better response history
- better intro completion history
- stronger path confidence
- better context fit by department or goal
- lower decay and staleness

## Good company-connector view

A strong company connector view should answer:

1. who inside the company is most reachable
2. which connector should make the ask
3. what evidence makes that route believable
4. whether the account is warming up or going dormant

## Follow-on actions

- open the strongest target path
- compare the fastest connector and most reliable connector
- turn the best route into a request, not a generic account note
- add the company to a [Collection](/workspace/collections) when it belongs to a repeatable workflow
